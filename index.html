Pitfalls & Red Flags

Click any item to expand details. Sorted by severity.

Critical  Ramp B Deck Replacement — PMD2 Not Prepared ▶
The original RRM recommended a concrete overlay for Ramp B (35A117). The Feb 2026 progress report states PMD2 is "being assembled" for a full deck replacement — a major scope escalation. This PMD has not yet been prepared, meaning the scope change is not formalized, no additional fee has been negotiated, and the schedule impact is unknown. Deck replacement is significantly more complex and costly than overlay. This is the single biggest risk item on the project.
Critical  60% Submission Date = TBD ▶
The original CPM schedule targeted 60% plans around Oct 2025 and PS&E around Nov 2025. As of Feb 2026, the 60% submission date is listed as "TBD." The project is 10 months past NTP with no scheduled date for the next major deliverable. The design schedule needs updating per the progress report.
Critical  PMD1 Fee = $0 (Zeroed) ▶
The PMD1 added a 6th bridge (35A118) with significant additional scope: field view, RRM update, 11+ additional plan sheets, TMP updates. The fee proposal for this work shows all hours and costs zeroed out. Markosky is effectively doing this work without an approved fee — this creates cost risk and potential disputes.
High  Netting Removal Blocking Survey Access ▶
Existing safety netting on the bridges is preventing survey crews from accessing the structures. Survey data is needed for design. Until the netting is removed, design work cannot progress on certain elements. This is an external dependency that the design team cannot control.
High  Utility Coordination Not Started ▶
The progress report notes utility coordination has not started — 10 months into the project. Utility conflicts discovered late in design are one of the most common causes of construction delays and change orders. The bridges carry or cross utilities that need to be identified and protected or relocated.
Medium  ODOT Coordination Needed for Detour Routes ▶
The interchange sits at the WV-OH state line. Detour routes during construction may route traffic through Ohio, requiring ODOT coordination. This interstate coordination adds complexity and time to the MOT design process and has not been finalized.
Medium  Fee Was Cut $46K Before Work Started ▶
The fee was reduced from $508,102 to $462,144 (a 9% cut, 426 hours removed) before NTP, primarily in Structure Rehab hours. With scope increasing (6th bridge, Ramp B deck replacement), the reduced fee base may not be adequate. Watch for supplemental fee requests.
Medium  NTP Slipped 2 Months at the Start ▶
Original proposal assumed NTP of Feb 3, 2025. Actual NTP was Apr 4, 2025 — a 2-month slip before work even began. This suggests the project had contracting/approval delays from the start, which may recur with PMD2 approvals.

Unclear / Ambiguous Items

What is the actual target date for 60% submission?
The progress report simply says "TBD." There's no updated CPM schedule in the documents. Ask Markosky for a revised schedule showing the critical path to 60% and PS&E.
What is the cost impact of PMD1 (6th bridge)?
The fee proposal for PMD1 is zeroed out — there's no approved dollar amount for the additional work. It's unclear if Markosky is absorbing this cost, if a fee is being negotiated, or if this is an oversight.
What is the scope and cost of PMD2 (Ramp B deck replacement)?
PMD2 is described as "being assembled" but no details are available. The jump from overlay to deck replacement is a significant cost and schedule escalation that hasn't been quantified.
Who is responsible for netting removal, and when will it happen?
The netting is blocking survey access, but the documents don't specify who owns this task, what the cost is, or when it's scheduled. This is a critical-path blocker.
Has ODOT been contacted about detour routes?
The need for ODOT coordination on Ohio-side detours is mentioned but there's no indication it has started. Cross-state coordination can have long lead times.

What's Going Well

Field Work Went Smoothly
The May 2025 field inspection found "no major concerns beyond originally anticipated scope." The field crew (Dietrich & Bacho) completed assessment of all 5 original bridges in a single visit. The 6th bridge field view was also completed on schedule (Dec 11, 2025).
RRM Was Delivered On Time
The original RRM was submitted Jun 4, 2025 — within the NTP + 60 days target. The revised RRM (Jun 24) was turned around quickly after the Jun 18 progress meeting, showing good consultant responsiveness.
Scope Is Well-Documented
The project narrative, RRMs, and PMD provide thorough documentation of scope. Repair recommendations are itemized per bridge with specific repair methods. The document trail is clear and traceable.
No Surprises on Original 5 Bridges
Field conditions on the original 5 bridges matched expectations from the scope of work meeting. The only scope additions were minor: concrete protective coating for substructures and Ramp C bearing reset.
PMD1 Process Worked Correctly
The addition of the 6th bridge (35A118) followed proper WVDOH process: PMD prepared → approved → NTP issued → field view completed. The documentation is thorough with full narrative and additional plan sheet breakdown.

Bottom Line

The Big Picture

This is a bridge rehabilitation project that started well — good field work, no surprises on original scope, timely RRM — but is now significantly behind schedule with expanded scope and unresolved blockers. Note: the 6th bridge (PMD1) was added to correct an omission, not scope creep — the schedule impact was anticipated.

  • Schedule: 36% complete after 10 months. Originally expected PS&E by Nov 2025 — now 60% plans don't even have a target date.
  • Scope growth: Started as 5-bridge rehab. Now 6 bridges — the 6th (35A118) was a bridge that should have been included originally and was added via PMD1 to correct the omission. Ramp B is also potentially jumping from overlay to full deck replacement (PMD2).
  • Money: Fee was already cut 9% before starting. PMD1 work has no approved fee. PMD2 cost is unknown.
  • Blockers: Netting removal → survey → design. Until netting comes down, design can't advance on certain elements.
  • Gaps: Utility coordination not started. ODOT detour coordination status unknown. Updated CPM schedule needed.

Questions to Ask in the Meeting

  2. What is the timeline and responsibility for netting removal?
  3. When will the PMD1 fee be finalized? Is Markosky working at risk?
  4. What is the preliminary cost estimate for PMD2 (Ramp B deck replacement)?
  5. When will utility coordination begin, and who is the point of contact?
  6. Has anyone contacted ODOT about detour routes through Ohio?
  7. Is the $462K fee still realistic given PMD1 + potential PMD2 scope additions?
